 coral reefs support a multibillion-dollar worldwide fishing industry, providing jobs for millions of people who live in coastal areas. reefs also supply food for people and are home to countless plants and animals. but lowland logging, rapid growth, and cyanide and dynamite fishing are placing this fragile world in jeopardy. the nature conservancy is working around the world to protect threatened marine environments, including kimbe bay in papua new guinea. the sapphire waters of this aquatic treasure host at least 860 species of fish and exquisite coral gardens.

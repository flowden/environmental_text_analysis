 to date, ci scientists in indonesia have attached satellite transmitters to the fins of 23 whale sharks. the tags are now providing an unprecedented look at these massive, mysterious fish — and you can follow them in near real-time. using this data, ci is collaborating with the georgia aquarium to ensure that whale shark tourism is managed sustainably and doesn’t adversely impact the health of the fish. + trackers gather data on their feeding and diving habits — much of it new to science + two of the sharks have dived nearly 6,000 feet deep — among the deepest dives observed for this species + migration habits are unique to each shark + anyone can follow 10 of the tagged sharks in the pacific

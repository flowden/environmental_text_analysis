 in january 2011, more than 500 volunteers descended upon helen wood park in mobile bay, ala., to kick off the ambitious 100-1000: restore coastal alabama effort by restoring an oyster reef. by july, conservancy staff was already seeing sediment accumulation, coastal marsh and seagrass recovery and an increase in birds and fish using the area. these early signs indicate that the living shoreline has already started fulfilling its ecological purpose, reinforcing the validity of pursuing future projects in the gulf region.

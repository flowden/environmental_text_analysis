 following the conservancy’s milestone acquisition of 161,000 acres of former finch, pruyn & co. lands, more than half of these forests continue to be managed for sustainable timber harvest. to date, 36,485 acres have been transferred to the adirondack park’s forest preserve and opened for public recreation. the conservancy committed $500,000 in adirondack park upper hudson recreation hub grants to encourage local communities to take advantage of new tourism and recreational economic opportunities. over the past 43 years, the conservancy has helped add 152,732 acres to the state forest preserve within the 6-million-acre adirondack park, a unique mixture of public and private lands, and has protected more than twice that acreage as sustainable working forests. nature.org/ar-adirondacks

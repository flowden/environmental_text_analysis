 the foundation supports numerous programs that inspire a love and understanding of nature in schoolchildren. priority is given to programs targeting urban and rural students in traditionally underserved areas. the objective is to engage kids at an early age– fueling their curiosity about the natural world when they are most inquisitive– and provide a continuum of opportunities that nurture their interest through college as they explore careers. through the foundation’s nature of learning program for example, schools and community groups receive grants enabling them to use america’s national wildlife refuges as outdoor classrooms and learning laboratories. the program promotes an interdisciplinary approach to teaching and partnerships that expand participation and impact. more than $436,000 was invested in 2005 through 35 nature of learning grants.

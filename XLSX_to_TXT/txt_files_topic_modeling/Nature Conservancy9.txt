 in the brazilian amazon smallscale family farmers have driven approximately onethird of the total loss of rainforest by cutting down trees to create farm fields and cattle pastures for subsistence and income but since 2012  and the government of the state of pará have been providing technical assistance and training to encourage farmers to grow cacao trees—the source of chocolate—to reforest degraded lands and to provide a more sustainable source of income last year however as harvest time neared covid19 hit brazil making it too dangerous to conduct inperson training the conservancy turned to a mobilephone based soalmessaging app to deliver howto videos to some 250 farmers in time to help with the harvest

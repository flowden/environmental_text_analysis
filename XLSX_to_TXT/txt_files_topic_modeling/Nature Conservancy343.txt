 the skagit river delta in western washington state is rich in wildlife it is a critical stop on the pafic flyway for migratory shorebirds and waterfowl and crual habitat for recovering salmon populations but the delta also boasts a familyfarming heritage this threeyear study supported by the conservancy and a coalition of university government and agricultural assoation partners seeks to discover how habitat rotation can be compatible with crop rotation with implications for similar estuary systems around the world part of the hypothesis is that farmers who rotate their land through threeyear cycles of controlled flooding that accommodates migratory shorebirds increase their yields and cut their costs by eliminating the need for pestides and chemical fertilizer
